State of New York,  
voters of district number one in the town of  
County of Allegany.  

The old school house pursuant to a call of  
citizens previously Thomas H. Langworthy being duly sworn says  
that he is a taxable inhabitant of district Number One in the  
town of Wellsville and was such at the time of his appointment  
by the trustees of such district to give notice as hereinafter  
mentioned that on the 21st day of October, 1859, he was appoint-  
ed and directed in writing by the trustees of said district to  
give notice to the taxable inhabitants of said district and all  
persons entitled to vote at district meetings therein of the per-  
meeting to be held on the third day of November, then next in  
pursuance of the foregoing notice in the manner provided by  
the law in the case of the formation of new school districts and  
said that in the pursuance of such appointment and direction he  
did give such notice to every other inhabitant of the district  
qualified to vote at district meetings therein as far as he  
could ascertain such inhabitants by reading the notice in the  
hearing of such inhabitant or in case of his absence from home-  
by leaving a copy of the substance thereof containing the time  
and place of such meeting at the place of his abode more than  
six days before the third day of November, 1859 and he further  
says that the number of inhabitants entitled to vote at district  
meetings of said district then and at the time appointed for  
the meeting was as near as could be ascertained one hundred and  
ninety two.  

Sworn and subscribed before me  
this thirteenth day of  
T. H. Langworthy.  
November, 1861.  

D. L. Vaughn, Justice  
of the Peace.  

L. D. Davis Chairman  
Sec. W. Russell Clerk
