STATE TEACHER'S ASSOCIATION

PROGRAM.

Monday Evening, July 1, 1895, 7.45 o'clock.

Preliminary meeting for organization.
Addresses of welcome to the Association—
Hon. Jacob Amos, Mayor of Syracuse.
Chancellor James R. Day, Syracuse University.
Rev. L. M. Clarke, Park Presbyterian Church.
A. B. Blodgett, Superintendent Syracuse Schools.
Response by the President.

Annual Educational address—
President J. G. Schurman, Cornell University.

Tuesday Morning, July 2, 9.30 o'clock.

Business meeting.
Paper: The Public Library and the Public School—
William R. Eastman, State Library, Albany.
Paper: Commercial Geography—
Assist. Supt. Gustave Straubenmüller, New York.
Paper: The Hygiene of the Eye—
Dr. Peter A. Callan, New York.
Paper: What the Uniform Examinations have Taught us—
A. C. Hill, State Department Public Instruction, Albany.

Tuesday Afternoon, July 2, 2 o'clock.

Paper: The Educative Function of Mathematics—
George K. Hawkins, State Normal School, Plattsburgh.
Paper: The Common Sense Primary Teacher—
Mary Louise Eastman, State Normal School, Cortland.
Paper: The Correlation of Studies, and their Relative Educational Values—
Samuel G. Williams, Ithaca.
