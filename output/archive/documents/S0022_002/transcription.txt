Fry's Geographies are universally recognized as representing an advance movement in education, but their crowning merit lies in the fact that they not only can secure better results than other books, but can make the pupils work more interesting and the teachers' work lighter.

The success of Fry's Geographies, which is literally unparalleled in the history of text-book publishing, shows a deep and widespread demand for the best ideas, methods, and books. It goes without saying that there is a little more skill and pains at the beginning, it requires a little more skill and pains instead of words from their text-books; but that is, of course, the only right way, to teach the pupils to get ideas from their text-books; but in a little time it becomes the easier and more pleasant.

The long list of towns throughout the State of New York that have adopted Fry's Geographies is a signal proof that the most cautious teachers, supervisors, and school men of the Empire State have been quick to recognize the sterling merits of these text-books. These Geographies are in harmony with the methods now followed by the best teachers of the subject. They are the first to embody these methods in a sensible, practical, and teachable form.
